Waterloo Park
50 Young St W, Waterloo, ON
Waterloo Park is an urban park situated in Waterloo, Ontario, Canada on land within Block 2 of the Haldimand Tract. Spanning 111 acres within the Uptown area of Waterloo, it opened in 1893 and is the oldest park in the city.

Clair Lake Park
Shakespeare Dr, Waterloo, ON
With over 22 acres of parkland and environmental reserve, the park boasts a combination of trails, a serene lake, and ample green areas. It encompasses diverse recreational activities, playgrounds, a well-equipped aquatic center, and designated picnic spots. The park stands proud with an array of family-friendly attractions catering to children and their parents, ensuring an enjoyable experience.

Brewmeister Green Park
155 King St S, Waterloo, ON
This fountain is located in what is now called Brewmeister’s Green Park, at the corner of King Street South and William Street. The land was set aside as a public square in 1855 through the John Hoffman survey and has been a public park ever since.

Laurel Creek Conservation Area Park
625 Westmount Rd N, Waterloo, ON
Laurel Creek Conservation Area, or simply Laurel Creek, is a park located in Waterloo, Ontario, Canada. The park is owned by the Grand River Conservation Authority. Laurel Creek is open year-round and offers several outdoor activities, such as swimming, campsites, picnic tables, and playgrounds.
